UnShrimp Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 1, 2026

UnShrimp (“we”, “the extension”) is a Chrome browser extension that helps users monitor seated posture using their device webcam. This policy explains what data the extension uses and how it is handled.

Summary

  • Webcam video is processed locally in your browser.
  • We do not upload, store, or sell photos or video from your camera.
  • Calibration data is saved only on your device.

Information the extension uses

When you choose to start monitoring on the UnShrimp monitor page:

  • Camera: The extension accesses your webcam to estimate upper-body pose landmarks. Frames are used in real time for posture detection and are not recorded or sent to our servers.
  • Pose data: Numeric landmarks and posture features are computed on your device to classify posture and show feedback.
  • Calibration baseline: If you calibrate, a small set of numeric features representing your normal seated posture is stored in your browser’s localStorage so the extension can compare future poses to your baseline.

What we do not collect

  • No account or login is required.
  • No analytics or advertising trackers are built into the extension.
  • No server receives your webcam images or video.

Notifications

If enabled, the extension may show Chrome notifications and play a local alert sound after sustained poor posture. You can turn sounds off in the extension settings.

Third-party components

The extension loads typography from Google Fonts when the monitor page is open. Pose estimation uses a local MediaPipe model shipped inside the extension package.

Data retention and deletion

Calibration data remains in your browser until you clear site data for the extension or remove the extension. Uninstalling the extension removes extension-local data from Chrome.

Children

UnShrimp is not directed at children under 13.

Wellness disclaimer

UnShrimp provides general posture awareness feedback only. It is not medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.

Changes

We may update this policy. The “Last updated” date at the top will change when we do.
